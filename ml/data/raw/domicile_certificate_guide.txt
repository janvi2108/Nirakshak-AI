Domicile / Residence Certificate Application Guide

WHAT IS A DOMICILE CERTIFICATE?
A domicile certificate (also called residence certificate) is an official document proving that a person is a permanent resident of a particular state or district. It is required for state government jobs, educational admissions under state quota, and various welfare schemes.

ELIGIBILITY:
- Must be a resident of the state for at least 3 years (varies by state; some states require 10-15 years for certain benefits)
- Must have permanent address in the state
- Birth in the state also qualifies for domicile

REQUIRED DOCUMENTS:
1. Aadhaar Card
2. Voter ID Card
3. Ration Card showing current address
4. Proof of residence for required years:
   - Electricity / Water / Gas bills (last 3 years)
   - Bank passbook with address
   - School / College leaving certificate showing address
5. Self-declaration affidavit on Rs. 100 stamp paper
6. Passport-size photographs (2 copies)
7. For women (after marriage): husband's domicile certificate + marriage certificate

HOW TO APPLY:
OFFLINE: Visit the Tehsil / SDM (Sub-Divisional Magistrate) office. Collect Form D-1. Submit filled form with documents. Application is forwarded to Patwari/Lekhpal for field verification.
ONLINE: Visit your state's e-district portal. Register, fill the form, upload scanned documents, pay fee online.

FEES:
- Application fee: Rs. 15 to Rs. 50 (varies by state)
- Affidavit on stamp paper: Rs. 100

PROCESSING TIME:
- Standard: 15 working days after field verification
- Express (where available): 7 working days
- Field verification by revenue official (Patwari): 7-10 days

VALIDITY:
Domicile certificates are generally valid for lifetime. However, some states issue certificates valid for 3 years for scheme-specific purposes.

COMMON REASONS FOR REJECTION:
1. Insufficient proof of residence duration
2. Address on Aadhaar not matching current application address
3. Affidavit not properly attested by Notary or Executive Magistrate
4. Applicant not found at address during field verification - ensure you are available

APPEAL PROCESS:
If rejected, file appeal with District Collector within 30 days. Attach rejection letter and additional supporting documents.
