Citizen Grievance and Complaint Redressal Process

HOW TO FILE A GRIEVANCE

ONLINE:
1. CPGRAMS (Centralized Public Grievance Redress and Monitoring System): pgportal.gov.in
   - Register with your mobile number
   - Select ministry/department
   - Describe grievance in detail
   - Attach supporting documents (max 2MB per file)
   - Note your grievance registration number
   
2. State portals: Most states have their own e-district grievance portal

OFFLINE:
- Visit nearest district office / tehsil office
- Submit written complaint to the concerned officer
- Get acknowledgment receipt with complaint number

---

GRIEVANCE CATEGORIES AND RESPONSIBLE DEPARTMENTS

Certificate Delays:
- Responsible: Revenue Department / District Magistrate office
- SLA: 7-15 working days (varies by certificate type)
- Escalation: Sub-Divisional Magistrate → District Magistrate → Commissioner

Officer Misconduct / Corruption:
- Responsible: Vigilance Department / Anti-Corruption Bureau
- File FIR at nearest police station for bribery demands
- Anti-Corruption Helpline: 1064 (in most states)
- Central Vigilance Commission: cvc.gov.in

Portal / Technical Issues:
- Responsible: IT Department of respective state
- Helpdesk email: Usually mentioned on the portal
- Common helpline: 1800-XXX-XXXX (check state portal)

Wrong Information in Documents:
- Responsible: Issuing authority (Revenue Department for most certificates)
- Submit correction request with supporting proofs
- Processing time: 5-10 working days

---

ESCALATION MATRIX (Certificate-related complaints)

Level 1: Office where application was submitted (3 working days)
Level 2: Tehsildar / Block Development Officer (5 working days)
Level 3: Sub-Divisional Magistrate / SDM (7 working days)
Level 4: District Magistrate / Collector (10 working days)
Level 5: Commissioner / Divisional Commissioner (15 working days)
Level 6: State Chief Secretary (20 working days)
Level 7: CPGRAMS (Central Government portal) for unresolved issues

---

IMPORTANT CONTACT NUMBERS

National Helplines:
- PM Helpline: 1800-11-0001 (Toll free)
- Women Helpline: 1091
- Child Helpline: 1098
- Anti-Corruption: 1064
- Civil Registration: 1800-180-1551

---

RIGHTS OF CITIZENS UNDER RTI ACT

Under the Right to Information Act 2005, you can:
- Request information about your application status
- Ask for certified copies of documents
- Inquire about reasons for rejection
- Know the names of responsible officers

RTI application fee: Rs. 10
Response time: 30 days (48 hours for life/liberty matters)
File RTI at: rtionline.gov.in
