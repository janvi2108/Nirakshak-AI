Death Certificate Application Guide

WHAT IS A DEATH CERTIFICATE?
A death certificate is an official document issued by the government recording the death of a person. It is mandatory for legal, financial, and administrative purposes including insurance claims, property transfer, bank account closure, and pension benefits.

WHO CAN APPLY:
- Immediate family members (spouse, children, parents, siblings)
- Legal heir or representative
- Employer (if death occurred at workplace)
- Hospital/medical facility (for in-hospital deaths)

REQUIRED DOCUMENTS:
1. Medical certificate of cause of death (Form 4A) from hospital or doctor
2. Proof of identity of the deceased (Aadhaar, voter ID)
3. Proof of identity of the applicant
4. Address proof of the deceased
5. For home deaths: affidavit from family member + two witnesses
6. For accidental deaths: copy of FIR and post-mortem report

FEES:
- Registration within 21 days: FREE
- After 21 days but within 30 days: Rs. 2
- After 30 days but within 1 year: Rs. 5
- After 1 year: Rs. 10 (requires court order)

HOW TO APPLY:
1. For hospital deaths: Hospital usually reports the death. Family must follow up with Municipal Corporation/Gram Panchayat to collect the certificate.
2. For home deaths: Report to local registrar within 21 days.
3. Online: Available on state civil registration portals and crsorgi.gov.in

PROCESSING TIME:
- 5-7 working days if all documents are in order
- Deaths registered after 1 year may take 30-45 days

USES OF DEATH CERTIFICATE:
- Claiming life insurance
- Transferring property/assets to legal heirs
- Closing bank accounts of the deceased
- Claiming government pension or gratuity
- Obtaining succession certificate from court
- Cancelling Aadhaar, voter ID, passport of deceased

HELPLINE: 1800-180-1551 (Civil Registration Helpline)
